A fish spends his life trying to put on pigeon feathers, believing that if he looks like them, one day he might fly. He competes, imitates, and struggles, forcing himself to fit into a world never meant for him, suffocating his own soul in the process. Poor fish, he never realises that he can swim in ways no pigeon ever could. Swimming was always his thing, yet he kept searching for wings.

He spent his life fighting for something that was never his, chasing dreams that could never lift him. All the while, what was truly his — his own ocean, his own way — waited quietly, patiently. But he never found it. He never discovered his Ikigai.

And you ? Have you found yours?
