When you are on the road to self-improvement — whether in your personal or professional life — there comes an intermediate phase where it becomes too difficult and demanding. This is the phase where you are trying to distance yourself from your bad traits and negative company, attempting to break old patterns that you know are toxic.

Then comes that continuous voice that keeps telling you to give up, go back — because it gets lonely, you lose company, you have to put in a lot of individual effort. It is like you are standing against yourself, required to crush your own soul.

But those who are strong in this — who have patience, resilience, and who continuously grind and improve minor aspects each day despite facing uncertainty and disappointments — can clear all the clouds and move beyond their toxic traits to become the best version of themselves.
